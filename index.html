TLP:Black — An Invitation-Only Extension to the Traffic Light Protocol

Abstract

The Traffic Light Protocol (TLP) has become a globally recognized standard for information sensitivity marking. This article proposes an extension, TLP:Black, to address scenarios where access is restricted not only by dissemination boundaries but also by audience selection at the point of disclosure. TLP:Black formalizes invitation-only trust circles, differentiating itself from TLP:Red by making audience pre-selection a defining characteristic.

Definition

TLP:Black: Information is disclosed strictly to an invitation-only group of participants. The information must not be shared outside the room, and access to the room itself is determined by explicit invitation. No redistribution is permitted.
